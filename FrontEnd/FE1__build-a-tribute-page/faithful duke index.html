Barack Obama

Here's a timeline of Obama's life:

Achievements

  • 1996 - Obama was elected to the Illinois Senate
  • 2000 - Lost a Democratic primary race for Illinois's 1st congressional district in the United States House of Representatives to four-term incumbent Bobby Rush by a margin of two to one.
  • 2001 - Supported Republican Governor Ryan's payday loan regulations and predatory mortgage lending regulations aimed at averting home foreclosures as co-chairman of the bipartisan Joint Committee on Administrative Rules.
  • 2003 - Became chairman of the Illinois Senate's Health and Human Services Committee when Democrats, after a decade in the minority, regained a majority.
  • 2004 - Elected to the U.S. Senate as the first African American to hold the office.
  • 2005 - Became the only Senate member of the Congressional Black Caucus.
  • 2006 - President Bush signed into law the Democratic Rep enacted with Obama as its primary sponsor.
  • 2007 - Sponsored an amendment to the Defense Authorization Act to add safeguards for personality-disorder military discharges.
  • 2008 - Became the first African American to be elected president.
  • 2009 - Reversed a Bush-era policy which had limited funding of embryonic stem cell research and pledged to develop "strict guidelines" on the research.
  • 2010 - Announced a planned change in direction at NASA, the U.S. space agency.
  • 2011 - Focused on themes of education and innovation, stressing the importance of innovation economics to make the United States more competitive globally.
  • 2012 - Won 332 electoral votes, exceeding the 270 required for him to be reelected as president.
